What Are Stars?

Stars are gigantic spheres of glowing gas, mostly hydrogen and helium, that generate energy through nuclear fusion in their cores. This process releases light and heat, which is why stars shine. They come in many types and sizes â€” from tiny red dwarfs to massive blue giants. A star's color tells us its temperature: blue stars are hottest, and red stars are coolest.

Stars are born in stellar nurseries called nebulae, live for millions to billions of years, and then die in dramatic ways â€” sometimes as white dwarfs, neutron stars, or black holes. Our Sun is a typical yellow star and has been burning for about 4.6 billion years.

The night sky is filled with stars of different brightness and colors. Some, like Polaris, help with navigation. Others, like Betelgeuse, are studied for signs of cosmic explosions. Each star tells a story of our universe's past and future.
