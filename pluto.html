About Pluto

Pluto is a dwarf planet located in the Kuiper Belt, a region beyond Neptune that is home to many icy objects. Once considered the ninth planet of our solar system, it was reclassified as a dwarf planet by the International Astronomical Union in 2006.

Pluto is known for its eccentric and tilted orbit, which sometimes brings it closer to the Sun than Neptune. It has five known moons, with Charon being the largest and closest to Pluto. The planet has a thin atmosphere composed mostly of nitrogen and methane.
