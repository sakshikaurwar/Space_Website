About Makemake

Makemake is a dwarf planet in the outer regions of the solar system, discovered in 2005. It is located in the Kuiper Belt, a region beyond Neptune that is populated with icy bodies and dwarf planets. Makemake is one of the largest known objects in the Kuiper Belt.

Unlike Pluto, Makemake has a very cold and dry environment. It has one known moon, MK 2, and a highly elliptical orbit that takes it far from the Sun. Makemake was named after the creator god of the Rapa Nui people of Easter Island.
