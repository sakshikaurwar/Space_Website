About Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun and one of the most unique in our solar system. It's an ice giant with a pale blue color due to methane in its atmosphere. Unlike other planets, Uranus rotates on its side, with its axis tilted over 90 degrees.

It has a cold atmosphere, faint rings, and at least 27 moons named after characters from literature. The planet was first discovered in 1781 by William Herschel, making it the first planet found with a telescope.
