About Eris

Eris is a dwarf planet located in the scattered disc, a distant region of the solar system. It was discovered in 2005 by astronomers at the Palomar Observatory. Eris is slightly smaller than Pluto, but it has a more distant orbit, making it one of the farthest known objects in our solar system.

Eris has one known moon, Dysnomia, and a surface covered with frozen methane. Due to its highly elliptical orbit, Eris moves from a distance closer to the Sun than Pluto, and it can be as far as 14 billion kilometers from the Sun.
