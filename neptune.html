About Neptune

Neptune is the eighth and farthest known planet in our solar system. It's an ice giant with deep blue coloring due to methane in its atmosphere. Known for its violent storms and supersonic winds, Neptune is one of the windiest places in the solar system.

The planet was discovered in 1846 and is about 17 times the mass of Earth. Its largest moon, Triton, orbits in the opposite direction and may have been a captured object. Neptune has faint rings and extreme seasonal variations due to its 165-year-long orbit.
